Forensic science technicians assist in criminal investigations by collecting and analyzing evidence.

  • Job outlook: Forensic science technician positions are projected to grow by about 14% from 2018 to 2028. There are currently 16,700 Forensic science technician positions as of 2018, with 2,400 new positions expected to be added by 2028.

  • Earnings: The median annual pay for Forensic Science Technicians is $58,230. Typically, those with more experience receive higher pay.

  • Common careers: Forensic Science Technician, Crime Scene Investigator, Private Investigator, Clinical Laboratory Technician.

  • Required education: The typical entry-level education for this field is a bachelor's degree. On the job training is generally required for those who investigate crime scenes and those who work in labs.
